How is it possible for physical system to misrepresent the state of their surroundings?

Only if we figure out how can we represent and misrepresent things, can we make sense of the idea that some physical systems can have beliefs and intentionality

## I. Natural Signs

The events that has correlation can indicate each other:

- Exapanding metal indicates a rising temperature
- dark shadows in X-ray indicate extensive decay

This indication can be connected by causal relations, or it can be mere regularities:

- A doorbell ringing indicates a person (instead of an animal) is at the door -- this is mere regularity: if animals learns to ring the bell, the indication is no longer reliable

> In other words, according to Drestke, natural signs are defined by correlation: if A correlates with B, then A is a natural sign of B.

Call these indicators "**natural signs**": How can natural signs misrepresent anything?

**Reply 1**: If type A correlates with type B, then every token of A "means" B, even if a token of B does not occur along with a token of A. In the case where a token of B does not occur, the token of A misrepresents B

- **Objection**: "Type-assiociated meaning does not help us understand misrepresentation unless the individual tokens of that type have the type-associated meaning"; If a natural indicator x does not occur with y, then x simply does not mean y.

> [!remark]
> I think Drestke's is simply begging the question here. What do you mean by "token of that type having the type-associated meaning"? Can't I say they just do because they are tokens of that type?
> An alternative objection might be that, a token can belong to so many types, and types can be correlated with so many other types, so what the token could represent would explode. It may turn out that the token is representing everything that is present and misrepresenting everything that is absent. But when we actual mean anything, there is a specific (though possibly vague) thing we are trying to represent. Therefore, something more needs to be said.

> Example for multi-purpose indicators:
> 
> **Camera sensor → infrared detector, and sometimes radiation detector**  
> Digital camera sensors are designed for **visible-light imaging**, but silicon image sensors are also sensitive to **near-infrared**, which is why many cameras include IR-cut filters. Researchers have also shown that CMOS camera sensors can respond to **ionizing radiation**, though not very well compared with purpose-built detectors.

## 2. Functional meaning

**feul gauge example**: when things are working properly, then the needle position indicates the fuel level; But if there's a malfunction, the needle may point to empty while the tank is full.

Functional Meaning
: d's being G means~f~ that w is F = d's function is to indicate the condition of w, and the way it performs this function is, in part, by indicating that w is F by d's being G

the needle pointing to empty means~f~ the tank is empty because the needle's function is to indicate the fuel level 

**Problem**: How do we naturalize the concept of "function"?

**Dog example**: For custom officers, the dog's barking indicates the presence of marijana. When the dog barks at an innocent box of herbs, we say the dog misrepresents the herbs as marijana. But this is because we intend the dog to detect marijuana, not herbs. Without appealing to our intended purpose we cannot say the dog misrepresents anything.
