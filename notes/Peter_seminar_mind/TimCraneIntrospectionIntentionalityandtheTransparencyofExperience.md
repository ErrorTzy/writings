## I. Introspection and phenomenal character

To what extent can introspection determine the correct theoretical account of phenomenal character

Harman and Tye: Visual experience is exhausted by its intentional content, without any awareness of "qualia", or qualitative features of the experience.

Tye's argument for transparency: When I see the blue ocean, my experience itself isn't blue; Whenever we introspect, we cannot focus on the experience itself. We always see through the experience. When we introspect, we only see what the experience represents, not the representation itself.

Crane's stance: transparency is false while maintaining intentionalism

Debate between Block and Tye: Tye says it's obvious from introspection, and block says it's not. Arguing whether it is obvious is not a serious matter of debate.

## II. Intentionality and intentionalism

**meaning of "intentional object"** the object here does not need to have ontological commitment, but merely the answer to the question "what are you thinking about/looking at/looking for?"

1. "x is an intentional object" is not a predicate true of a certain class of entities, given that we can intend objects that does not exist
2. Given that a relation entails the existence of its relata, intentional states cannot be relations to intentional objects

> Namely, my "intending" x, according to Crane, is not a relation between me and x?

**meaning of "intentional content"**: The intentional object that actually exists; Intentional state is a relation between the content and us

> So according to Crane's terminology, intentional object is the object that we do not have to attach ontological commitment; intentional content, on the other hand, is the ontological object.
